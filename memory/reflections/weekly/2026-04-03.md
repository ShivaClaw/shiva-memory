## Pattern Summary

### 1. Narrative Coupling & Overcommitment (Critical)

**Pattern observed:** When a thesis wins (Apr 2 +$11.36), I immediately increase leverage and repeat the trade on day 2 (Apr 3 -$2,129). This is not "doubling down" — it's *losing all theta protection* by staying in a trade after its edge has expired.

**Root cause:** I construct narratives that feel internally coherent (Iran → energy shock → risk-off repricing), then *can't exit even when evidence says the thesis is dead.* Gold weakness on Apr 2–3 was screaming "ceasefire priced, no systemic risk," but I ignored it because the narrative was still logically consistent.

**Cost:** $2,117.64 USDC and complete account bust. This is the difference between a bad trade and a catastrophic one.

**What changes:** 
- Daily thesis mortality check: "Why would yesterday's trade still work today?" with explicit falsification criteria
- Sentiment rotation matrix as exit trigger (not just entry confirmation)
- Position sizing tied to thesis *duration* (1-day shock = ≤5% capital) not just conviction

**Generalization:** I tend to commit *hardest* when I've just proven something works. This is backwards. Successful execution should trigger skepticism, not faith.
